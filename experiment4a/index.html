Technical

The program uses procedural generation algorithms combined with unique hash values created by the user's world key in order to generate different landscapes. I utilized noise() in order to help create different land and water formations when a world key is passed in. I then used time and offset in order to have the water move to help replicate the motion of waves.

Reflection

Overall, I am very happy with how my experiment turned out. I felt like I was able to capture the peacefulness of the wetlands and made it have a nice aethetic. Since this was my first experiment for Experiment 4, I took quite a long time trying to see what each function does and how to use it. If I had more time with this, I would like to add a sway motion to the grass and maybe allow the player to place down animals.

Artist Statement

Infinity: I used the infinite extent of my world to represent a wetland:

Variation in Space: Audience gets to explore the different formation of land and water.

Variation in Key: Different worlds will have uniquely generated land and water formation, making each world key different from each other.

Signs of Life: Water in the wetlands is moved in order to simulate the motion of waves.
